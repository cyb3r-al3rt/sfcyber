Dark Web Exposure Report

Find out if your business credentials, customer data, or intellectual property are already compromised.

Who This Is For

Businesses that need to know if their data has been exposed in a breach, including:

  • Companies with online customer accounts
  • Organizations handling sensitive client data
  • Businesses concerned about employee password reuse
  • Firms that have experienced suspicious account activity

What We Monitor

1. Company Email Domains

We search dark web forums, paste sites, and breach databases for your company's email addresses and associated passwords.

2. Executive Credentials

Focused monitoring on C-level and privileged user accounts that pose the highest risk if compromised.

3. Customer Data Leaks

Checks for exposed customer records, payment card data, or personal information tied to your business.

4. Intellectual Property

Searches for leaked source code, proprietary documents, or trade secrets that may have been exfiltrated.

Deliverables

  • Exposure Report: Detailed summary of all discovered exposures with redacted samples
  • Risk Assessment: Severity ratings for each finding based on data type and exposure age
  • Remediation Steps: Immediate actions like forced password resets, MFA enablement, and monitoring setup
  • Ongoing Monitoring Options: Guidance on setting up continuous dark web alerts

Process & Timeline

  1. Day 1: Discovery call and scope definition
  2. Days 2-3: Dark web monitoring and data collection
  3. Day 4: Analysis and report preparation
  4. Day 5: Report delivery and remediation planning call

Total timeline: 3-5 business days from kickoff to final report.

Pricing

Investment starts at ₹20,000 for initial assessment. Ongoing monitoring available as an add-on.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far back do you search?

We search historical breach databases going back 10+ years and monitor current dark web activity.

What if you find exposed passwords?

We provide immediate guidance on forcing password resets and implementing multi-factor authentication to prevent account takeover.

Is ongoing monitoring included?

The initial report is a point-in-time assessment. We can set up continuous monitoring as an ongoing service.
