Basic Policy Pack

The "get out of jail free card" for compliance, insurance, and peace of mind.

Who This Is For

Businesses that need formal, written security policies to satisfy external requirements and protect themselves legally:

  • Growing companies that have surpassed startup phase and need to formalize operations
  • Government contractors or B2B vendors who must prove compliance to partners
  • Healthcare, legal, or financial firms with strict data protection requirements (HIPAA, CCPA, etc.)
  • Businesses applying for cyber insurance — policies often pay for themselves by securing lower premiums

What's Included

1. Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

Defines how employees can use company-owned equipment, network resources, and data. Sets clear expectations and reduces risky behavior.

2. Information Security Policy (ISP)

Outlines your overall approach to security, including roles, responsibilities, incident response procedures, and enforcement.

3. Data Breach Response Plan (DBRP)

Step-by-step playbook for what to do if a breach is suspected or occurs. Defines roles, escalation paths, and notification requirements.

4. Employee Acknowledgment Forms

Templates for employees to sign confirming they've read and understood the policies—critical for enforcement and liability protection.

Why This Matters

These policies solve a critical business and legal problem, not just a technical one:

  • Cyber insurance requirements: Insurance applications increasingly demand proof of formal security policies
  • Legal protection: If you're ever sued or audited, these policies prove you took reasonable steps to protect data
  • Vendor compliance: Many larger companies require vendors to have these policies (SOC 2, ISO 27001 audits)
  • Employee clarity: Sets clear expectations and provides a basis for consistent enforcement

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we use generic templates from the internet?

You could, but they won't be tailored to your specific industry, data types, or compliance needs. Insurance companies and auditors can spot generic templates—customized policies demonstrate real commitment.

Do we need to update these policies annually?

Yes. We recommend annual reviews to ensure policies reflect current practices, new regulations, and changes in your business operations.

What if we already have some policies in place?

Great! We can review your existing policies, identify gaps, and provide updates or additions as needed at a reduced scope.
